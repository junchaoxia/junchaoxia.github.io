I am research software engineer at Princeton University. I have both roles of research decorvery and software development for most of my projects, from early physical systems in the context of condensed matter and statistical physics to recent biological macromolecules for health problems. My long-term interests in developing algorithms and software to perform state-of-art computing in structural biology, computational biophysics and chemistry, statistical and condensed matter physics are listed as but not limited to: computational CryoEM, NMR calculations, accelerated sampling methods in high-dimensional space, force field developments, implicit and explicit solvation models, and free energy calculations using homogeneous high performance clusters or heterogeneous distributed systems.
